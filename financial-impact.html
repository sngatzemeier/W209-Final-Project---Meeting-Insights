Are more meetings are Good?

Think again they cost your firm!

Companies waste millions of dollars organizing and having meetings, finding time-slots, waiting for participants to arrive, discussing irrelevant topics and divergent ideas, multitasking during meetings, etc.

It’s a well-known fact that more than 50% of the meetings are ineffective. Many hours are wasted each year just on standing weekly meetings, which then have a further ripple effect on other activities. The problem is getting worse. Time devoted to discussions has increased each year since 2008, partly because of technology, organizing meetings has become a lot easier.

The question is, how much do these meetings cost? Try a calculator created by The Harvard Business Review .

Say five people have a meeting lasting 30 minutes and each person involved earns a salary of $60,000. That costs the company $105. Or, say, an hour-long session for ten people with an average salary of $80,000. The cost is $560. An hour-long meeting for a whole department (~30 people) where the average salary is $50k costs $1,015.

So ask yourself: Is your meeting resulting in decisions that generate enough revenue to make it worth it?

When salaries are high, the results can be jaw-dropping. A weekly executive standing meeting can cost up to $15 million, even though a lot of what went on didn’t translate into noticeable improvements in services, operations, and products.
